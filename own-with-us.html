    Frequently Asked Questions

    Answers to Your Most Common Questions

Can I buy property in my own name even if I'm based overseas (OFW)?

Yes. Filipino citizens can buy and own property in the Philippines, even if they're living or working abroad.

What are the requirements for OFWs to buy property?

  • Valid government ID or passport
  • Proof of income or employment (contract, payslip, remittance records)
  • Special Power of Attorney (SPA) if a representative will sign on your behalf
  • TIN (Tax Identification Number)

Can I buy property and put it under a family member’s name?

WYes, but it's important to consider the legal and inheritance implications. Always consult with a lawyer before doing so.

What’s the difference between pre-selling and ready-for-occupancy (RFO) units?

Pre-selling: Lower price, more flexible terms, but longer wait time
RFO: Can move in or rent out right away, but often more expensive

Can I get a housing loan as an OFW?

Yes. Many Philippine banks and developers offer OFW housing loan programs. Requirements vary per bank and may include:

  • Employment contract
  • Valid visa or work permit
  • Proof of income
  • Co-borrower or attorney-in-fact in the Philippines

Can I buy property with a spouse?

Yes. Under Philippine law, property bought during marriage is usually considered conjugal property (shared), unless under a prenuptial agreement.

Is it possible to invest in property for rental income or resale?

Absolutely. Many Filipinos now invest in condotels, Airbnb units, or vacation homes to earn passive income, especially in tourist areas like El Nido, Baguio, or Tagaytay.
